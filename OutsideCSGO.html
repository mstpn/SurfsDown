Related - Outside CS:GO

There comes a point where too much CounterStrike is too much, regardless of the game mode. Sometimes you want to keep playing games, and sometimes you want to kick back and relax. The closest link that I find to surfing is racing. The sense of speed, the perfection of movement in order to achieve better times. I've added some links to racing games you may enjoy below. There are also Formula 1 videos, as I find it the perfect sport for the speed enthusiast.

Racing Games

Racing games have two main flavours: realistic and non-realistic. I'll go over some options for both below.

Sim Racing

Sim games such as iRacing bring you as close as technology allows it to being in a racecar. So much so that professional drivers play them for fun! Here is a video of Formula 1 driver Lando Norris watching another F1 driver, Max Verstappen, racing in a game version of the 24 Hours of Spa endurance event. Check out iRacingfor more information on how you can get behind the wheel of a virtual racecar.

TrackMania

On the other end of the realism spectrum we have TrackMania. Despite being nothing like driving a real car, the skill required for high end maps in this game is breathtaking. See what I mean below, and check out one of the many versions of TrackMania on Steam if you're interested in trying it out. Don't worry, it starts out WAYYY easier than what the lunatic below is doing.

Formula 1

Formula 1. The pinnacle of motorsport. The 20 best drivers in the world all go head to head in the fastest cars that engineers can create. The speed and acceleration is unreal. Watch the clip of the pole lap of legend Ayrton Senna as he rips around the narrow streets of Monaco in 1990. And watch the 7-time world champion Michael Schumacher duke it out in the rain with rival Mika Hakkinen around the Nurburgring in 2000.
